Ocean is asking for help

What’s the most common element found in the ocean?
Fishes or sands?

Disappointingly, the answer is plastic.

The severity of marine plastics pollution far exceeds our imagination. According to a study conducted by the University of Georgia, 18 billion pounds of plastic trash winds up in our ocean each year.[1]To put that in perspective, the amount of trash is “enough to cover every foot of coastline around the world with five full trash bags of plastic…compounding every year.”[2]

According to statistics, there are 25 trillion pieces of plastic debris in the ocean, which is mainly discarded human rubbish.[3] 80% of trash in the ocean is from land-based sources, including individuals, industry and improper waste management/infrastructure.[4]

Some people have understandings about marine plastic garbage that it is just plastic bags, beverage bottles, etc. visible to our eyes. However, besides the plastic trash we can easily recognize, there is also much tiny plastic or microplastic rubbish in the ocean, which can lead to more serious problems. Research estimates anywhere from 15 to 51 trillion particles of floating microplastic in our oceans, weighing between 205-520 million pounds.[5] This includes plastic microbeads (used as exfoliates in some personal care products) and synthetic fibers, both of which are too small to be filtered out by many wastewater treatment plants.

trash picked on coast

The situation on the coast is almost as bad as in the ocean. The plastic trash, both normal size ones and micro ones, are all over the beach. The plastic in the marine areas takes a long, long time to decompose. And the great amount of them is enough to circle the Earth 400 times. [6]

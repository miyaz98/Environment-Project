CLEANUPers

The stories of CLEANUPers

CONTINUE

“I started to organize the cleanup group because I like to take my dogs to walk on the beach.”Mary says. “So it is a personal connection, I think it’s most people to start any kind of cleanup. It’s a personal reason at first, and then it comes to how to help the environment more.”

Volunteers from different ages and countries gather at Albany Beach, to undertake this non-profit work together. The purposes of their participation may vary from person to person, but are inseparable from the care about the environment.

Staci, a cleanuper who has done cleanup for four years, says “when I look around and get involved in nature. The plastic around just shocked me, and I think the cleanup is just an issue for me.”

In addition to cleanup, they also carry out some environmentally-related entertainment activities. One of the fun things they do is that Zach Pine, an environmental artist, always offers a workshop on making sand globes (big, melon-sized balls of sand) for beach cleaners.

“If you say something to a person, like ‘you should care more’, ‘you should not throw so much plastic.’, it is not going to change anything.” when is asked about why he invented this art, Zach says. “Many people have known the marine problem, but they feel healthy. The pollution is in one place, they are in another place.”

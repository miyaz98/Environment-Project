The Cleanup Meetup was founded in 2013. Since 2013, the organization has been clearing three large beaches at Albany Beach. In 2017, they also screened most of the beaches to remove small plastics.

Mary, one of the founders of the organization, told us that at first, they were just walking dogs along the coast. When they were walking along the coast, they found that marine pollution was very serious. So they organized their organization to clean up the marine pollution. So they named the organization Albany Waste Landfill Dog Owners Group and Friends (ALDOG). They organize at least three beach cleanups per year. As researchers have documented, deposits of plastics and other debris on beaches, including very small particles, may have been caused by the San Francisco Bay Stream.

Albany Beach

It is worth mentioning that ALDOG organized the East Bay’s first big beach microplastics cleanup (October 2017) with the filtration screens. That day, 115 volunteers removed 2,440 pounds of debris from the beach in just six hours. People of all ages and professions participate in the cleanup group, and some will bring their children to join together. All volunteers are happy to participate in such events. One organization Staci says, An hour or two spent cleaning & protecting our food chain is quality time with the Anthropocene era.

CLEANUPers are working

The main task of the cleanup group is to collect plastic waste from the beach. Volunteers who join the group just take anything they think will work to do the cleanup(buckets, gloves, etc.) to pick up the large, visible plastic litter on the beach, and use sieves to filter tiny ones. As Mary says, people can see bottles, cans, bags on the beach easily, but it’s hard to find and pick up the micro ones. Microplastics are very small pieces of plastic which are less than 5 mm in length, and have an extremely serious harm to marine environment and organisms. In order to clean up the microplastic waste, the cleanup group uses special static-charge filtration screens (invented by Marc Ward of Sea Turtles Forever) that allow removal of very small microplastics. The tools help them to cleanup more efficiently.
